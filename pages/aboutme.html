My Past

I was born in Nairobi, Kenya on December 17, 1990. My father is a doctor and my mother works intelligence for the government. At the age of three my brother mother and I moved to D.C for better education. My father remained in Nairobi. Since then I have spent my schol years in America, and my summers in Kenya. I feel blessed to have lived such a diverse life, and I take pride in giving back to the community.


Web Development

My journey into web development is traced back to when I graduated college. Immediately following graduation, I went to visit my father in Kenya, and ended up climbing Mt. Kilimanjaro. It was at this moment that I knew I wanted to live by the mountains. I came back to the states packed up my bags, and moved to Colorado. I intended to spend the next two years working in physical rehabilitation and fitness before going to grad school. One of my clients at the time happened to be a web developer. Having always had a curiousity for technology I began asking him questions about it. It started off as a hobby, I had no intention of pursuing it full time. However, eventually I found that I enjoyed Web Development more than I enjoyed rehabilitation. This led me to take the full plunge and pursue an immersive Full Stack bootcamp at Galvanize.


My Future

My goal is to find the company that sees the same potential in me, that I see in myself. I thrive in new technologies and want to be exposed to a wide range of tasks. Ideally I will be in a Full Stack or Back-end position. TDD is a must, as well as solid mentorship. I thrive around people, and I am very confident that I will have a positive impact at whatever company I end up at.
